Increasing adoption through a simplified onboarding experience

Team

1 designer, 1 PM, 1 dev

Timeline

2 months

Summary

Fastly had a checkout process that was cumbersome and error-prone. I created a new experience that led to over 300 new paid accounts per month.

Customers couldn’t complete the checkout process

The checkout process was split between two pages. First, the user had to enter a tax address. Then they had to navigate to an entirely different page and enter their payment details. Finally, after they saved their details, they had to check a box to agree to terms and hit submit.

This led to hundreds of customers with payment information on file but not being billed simply because they never clicked submit.

The checkout process also did not comply with legal requirements and could have landed Fastly in legal trouble.

Old checkout page

How we fixed the problems

We combined the tax address form and the payment information form to make it easier for the user to upgrade their account.

Improving the form validation made it impossible for users to get stuck in the state where their information was saved but was not being billed.

We also worked with legal to ensure we were disclosing the correct information.

New upgrade page - address step

Identifying the issue

During an audit of our account pages, I identified the checkout pages as an opportuinty for improvement. I jumped into Pendo to look for some key metrics. I also asked for SQL reports from my backend engineers to be run for data that's not in Pendo.

I found that 22% of customers failed to get through the upgrade process. I also found hundreds of customers who were stuck with payment information added but were not getting billed.

New upgrade page - payment step

Competitive analysis

Next, we looked to see what our competitors were doing. They all price and package their products differently so the results were varied.

However, we found a trend that most took a “Shopify-like” approach to the checkout process where you keep the entire flow on one screen.

Competitive analysis FigJam Board

Designing, testing, and aligning with stakeholders

The design was then tested internally with Fastly employees. We also leveraged usertesting.com and ran unmoderated usability tests.

Once people were able to find the upgrade page it was easy to complete the process. However, many users struggled to find where they needed to go to upgrade.

This was a key finding that informed future projects, but to keep the scope small, we decided to take that problem on as a next step.

Results

  • Satisfied legal requirements
  • Currently produces over 300 new upgrades a month
  • Eliminated the possibility for users to have billing information on file but not be billed
  • Made the page mobile-friendly so you can complete checkout from any device
